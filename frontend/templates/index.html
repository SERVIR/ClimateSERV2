ClimateSERV - Where climate data lives

Climate data and tools to enhance study, analysis and decision making

Background

ClimateSERV allows development practitioners, scientists/researchers, and government decision-makers to visualize and download historical rainfall data, vegetation condition data, and 180-day forecasts of rainfall and temperature to improve understanding of, and make improved decisions for, issues related to agriculture and water availability.

What you can do with ClimateSERV

As a student you can use ClimateSERV data to help in your thesis. For example you could use Integrated Multi-satellitE Retrievals for GPM (IMERG) dataset which provides near-realtime global observations of rainfall at 10km resolution, to estimate total rainfall accumulation from storm systems and quantify the intensity of rainfall and flood impacts from tropical cyclones and other storm systems in your specific area of study.

Because ClimateSERV has historical data you can also drill down through the data to get statistical information about the study area over time in order to compare, contrast, and model different scenarios. Using this data you can download and print charts to help support your thesis.

As a Student  |  As a Researcher  |  As a Decision Maker

As a researcher you could use NDVI and rainfall data from ClimateSERV to identify the effects of interventions by selecting two areas of interest, one control with no intervention and another that has experienced intervention. Each of these areas you would perform an on-demand comparative analysis which would result in a chart for each. Those charts could help you highlight the differences of the two areas in terms of vegetation health, derived growing season lengths and rainfall. You can also download the summary data table as well as the full dataset for the period specified.

With the result of this analysis, you could help showcase the utility of the intervention programs and to provide an objective rationale for expansion of those intervention programs.

As a Student  |  As a Researcher  |  As a Decision Maker

As a decision maker you could use the rainfall data from ClimateSERV to help predict droughts, estimate crop yields, and much more. ClimateSERV provides data that you could use to accurately assess how severe a drought will be, how it compares to past droughts, and its potential effect on crop yields. This fact is due to the long historical archive of data offered through the system. It is important to place an evolving dryer-than-normal season into historical context in order to analyze the severity of rainfall deficits.

Being able to instantly narrow your statistical calculations to your specific area of interest and time period gives you fresh, as well as historical data at a moments notice.
